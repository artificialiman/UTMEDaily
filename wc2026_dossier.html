04 · Peak zone
Press-to-transition midfield engine

Portugal's peak zone is the central midfield press-and-win corridor (35–65m from goal). When Vitinha, João Neves and Bernardo are all operating together in this band, they create a trap: Vitinha invites the press, J. Neves provides the immediate tackle, Bernardo receives the won ball and plays it forward before the opposition can reorganise. This 3–6 second sequence is the moment Portugal is hardest to stop in the world.

04 · Peak zone
Defensive shape — the Atlas block still world-class

Morocco's peak zone under Regragui was the deep defensive block — arguably the best in the world at WC 2022. Under new coach Ouahbi the system evolves toward a high-press 4-3-3 with a mid-block fallback. The best version of Morocco 2026 is a hybrid: press high to force errors, then drop into the disciplined 4-4-2/4-5-1 block that frustrated France, Portugal and Spain in Qatar. The defensive line coordination (Aguerd + Masina as CB pair) is still elite. Hakimi in defensive mode is still the best right-back in terms of combining pressing and tracking runs.

The home-tournament factor is real: no team handles a hostile crowd environment better than the host.

Tournament verdict
Portugal

The most complete of the three teams across all five criteria. The pausa engine (Vitinha + Rúben Neves) is tournament-best, the iso quality (Leão) is world-class, and the multi-zone scoring threat (Ronaldo + Ramos) is unique. The ceiling is a top-4 finish. The floor risk: over-reliance on Ronaldo as the tactical reference point creates rigidity; a disciplined deep-block (Morocco-style) can neutralise Portugal's peak zone.

Morocco

The most defensively cohesive team of the three. Their peak defensive zone is the closest to world-best. The coaching change is the single biggest risk — a new system 3 months before a World Cup introduces unknown fragility. If Ouahbi's high-press 4-3-3 beds in, Morocco can be devastating (Brazil in Group C will define whether this system works). The Brahim + El Kaabi + Hakimi triangle going forward is underrated.

USA

The most tactically limited of the three on paper. The pausa gap is real and significant. However: Pochettino's coaching quality, the home-tournament advantage, and the group draw (Paraguay, Australia, Türkiye) all maximise USA's ceiling in the group stage and potentially the round of 32. If Reyna is fit, the dynamic upgrades considerably. The floor (a group exit) and ceiling (quarterfinals) are both more realistic than they appear on a pure talent assessment.
