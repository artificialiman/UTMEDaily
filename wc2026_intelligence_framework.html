The framework — how to read every team tab
Step 1 — The system drives everything
Coach picks the system. System selects which players' combinations matter. Everything else is context.

We are not analysing individual player quality in isolation. We are reading what the coach has consistently prioritised — which 2–3 player combinations are so automatic they run without thinking — and building our predictions from that core outward. France play around Mbappé not Dembélé. Argentina's midfield exists to protect and serve Messi's movement radius. England's shape exists to maximise Kane's drop-deep playmaking and Bellingham's late arrivals. Spain's press exists because the Rodri–Pedri axis reads the game 2 passes ahead of everyone else. This is the lens.

The three-layer decision system
Layer 1 — Criteria observation
Which criteria fire for this team in this match?

Five criteria from the dossiers: Scoring Threat · Iso · Gravity · Peak Zone · Pausa. Each match, you check which criteria are structurally present — not "this team is good" but "this specific mechanism will operate."

Key: A criterion only fires if the opposition CANNOT structurally prevent it. If they can, the criterion is neutralised and the market it would have driven disappears.
Layer 2 — Opposition check
Is this team specifically equipped to stop that threat?

This is the most important step and the one most bettors skip. A team with three outside-box threats is dangerous — unless the opposition has a deep 6-man defensive block that concedes zero space outside the box. The threat exists only if the opposition's structure cannot address it.

How the 6 tickets are structured
Each ticket = 1 outright winner bet + path-confirmed prop legs

Six separate tickets. Each is built around one team winning the tournament. The outright is the anchor — the longest odds leg. The prop bets are the near-certainty legs that add multiplier value without speculative risk. The goal: the prop legs are so high-confidence that even if the outright fails, the prop return is positive. The outright win on any ticket is pure upside.

Ticket structure
Leg 1 (anchor): Team to win tournament
Leg 2–3: Group stage near-certainties (BTTS/Over)
Leg 4–5: Path-confirmed R32/R16 props
Leg 6 (optional): Final matchup prediction if path is clear
Prop selection rule
Only use props where 3+ criteria fire AND the opposition check passes. Never include a prop where a single elite defender or system could neutralise the entire threat. The prop must be structurally inevitable, not just likely.
Arb layer
Check the outright winner odds across Bet365, BetMGM and Kalshi. If the spread between best and worst is >15%, the arb gap is exploitable. Place the outright at the best-priced book; place the counter (e.g. "not to win") at a second book if the combined implied probability is <100%.

Coach system — Deschamps' final chapter
System: 4-2-3-1. Everything converges on Mbappé's movement corridor.

Deschamps has never deviated from one core truth in 13 years: pick the 10 players who make Mbappé's specific movement pattern work, not the 10 best players. The double pivot (Tchouaméni + Zaïre-Emery) shields the defence so the rest of the team can funnel ball to Mbappé's left channel. Dembélé right / Mbappé left is the output axis. Thuram or Olise is the third attacker adjusting to the opposition. The system sacrifices creative midfield flair (Camavinga left out entirely) for structural security and Mbappé maximisation. At his final WC, Deschamps knows exactly what he's building.

Coach system — Martínez's midfield-first world
System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1 hybrid. Best midfield in the tournament. Vitinha–João Neves–Bruno Fernandes triple axis.

Portugal won the Nations League 2025 with this system. Martínez builds from the midfield out: Vitinha and João Neves (both PSG, Champions League winners 2024-25) provide the press resistance and ball-moving quality, while Bruno Fernandes provides the final-third creation and set-piece threat. Bernardo Silva floats between midfield and attack. Ronaldo (41) leads the line — his role is finalisation rather than creation. The system serves Ronaldo's remaining strengths: aerial threat, set-piece conversion, and the penalty box presence that demands a constant defensive assignment. You noted it yourself: Ronaldo's system is defined by who wins the box fouls, who crosses, who creates the conditions for his penalty-area moments. That's Nuno Mendes (cross), Bruno Fernandes (set-pieces + through balls), Bernardo Silva (combinations).

Primary chain — PSG midfield core
VI
Vitinha — deep creation engine
PSG · UCL winner · Portugal's most important midfielder
Receives deep, turns instantly, plays the progressive ball. His PSG partnership with João Neves means these two have played 2,000+ club minutes in the same midfield this season. Their national team combination is an extension of club rhythm, not a new relationship. Vitinha's pausa quality is elite — he controls Portugal's tempo and decides when to accelerate.
JN
João Neves — press + carry
PSG · 21 yrs · UCL winner · natural complement to Vitinha
The younger, more aggressive half of the PSG pairing. Where Vitinha controls, Neves presses. Where Vitinha distributes, Neves carries. Together they form what analysts have called the best young midfield partnership in European football. At national level, their relationship is already tested at the highest pressure: Nations League final (won vs Spain on penalties, 2025).
BF
Bruno Fernandes — attacking mid + set piece master
Manchester United · 31 · Portugal's scoring engine from midfield
The link between the PSG pair and Ronaldo's zone. Bruno Fernandes takes every set piece, every penalty, every free kick that creates Ronaldo's chances. His through-ball to Ronaldo's diagonal run has been Portugal's most dangerous combination for 4+ years of international football. At 31 he is at his national team peak.
Secondary chain — the Ronaldo service chain
NM
Nuno Mendes — the cross deliverer
PSG · 2025 Nations League Best Player · left back
The most in-form full-back in European football entering the tournament. His left-side driving runs and cross delivery are what creates Ronaldo's near-post opportunities. Ronaldo's header threat depends on Nuno Mendes getting free on the left. This combination has been rehearsed across qualifying, Nations League and club football for 3 years. Mendes won the Nations League Best Player award — he is not a support act.
CR
Cristiano Ronaldo — box presence finaliser
Al Nassr · 41 · 143 Portugal goals · 6th World Cup
At 41, Ronaldo's role is precisely defined: occupy the CB, win the aerial duel, convert from the penalty spot or the near-post header. He does not press. He does not carry. He is the destination of every chain that Bruno and Nuno Mendes build. Portugal's system converges on creating 3–5 Ronaldo-reachable situations per game and expecting him to convert 1–2. His last tournament, his last trophy chance.
Note: Rúben Dias (hamstring, March) is the significant injury concern. His defensive partnership with Gonçalo Inácio anchors Portugal's clean-sheet capability. Without Dias at full fitness, Portugal's defensive floor drops significantly and the whole system becomes more attack-dependent.
