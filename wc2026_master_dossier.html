Game state criteria — GS1 through GS4 New framework
GS1 · Double plus 1
Portugal's spatial coverage: how 22 players + ball changes their 7/11 dependency
Portugal's 4-3-3 nominally requires 7 of their 11 players in optimised positions for a clean shot opportunity. In practice their system compresses this through Vitinha's pausa — he holds the ball long enough (even 0.5s) for Leão's run to advance 4-5m, which is the difference between an open left channel and a covered one. The "plus 1" (the ball itself) matters most for Portugal when Vitinha switches play to Conceição on the right — the ball travelling 40m in under 1s forces the defensive shape to reposition faster than human sprint speed allows. Portugal's peak exploitation of the double-plus-1 spatial advantage: the diagonal switch from deep, where the ball crosses 5 defensive players in under 2 seconds while they can only move 3-4m in the same time.
Portugal's 7/11 path dependency reduces to ~5/11 when Vitinha switches to the weak side — the ball's speed removes two defensive blockers from the equation before they can reposition. This is the geometric mechanism behind why a long switch from Neves followed by Conceição's run is disproportionately dangerous relative to its xG from shot location alone.
GS2 · Pausa discretion
Vitinha's hold-release timing as simultaneous cost imposition on both teams
Vitinha's pausa discretion is the finest in this dossier numerically (93.7%, 2,413 passes). The discretion mechanism: he receives under pressure, his first touch buys 0.3-0.5 seconds of disguise, and the opposition pressing team must choose whether to commit the second presser. If they commit: Vitinha releases and the press is broken. If they don't commit: he carries into the next zone. Either decision costs the opponent energy — either a wasted sprint or a conceded 5-10m of territory. His Portuguese teammates understand this: their off-ball sprint timing is calibrated to his hold, so Portugal's runners begin their acceleration the moment Vitinha's body shape indicates release rather than hold. Against teams that press with 3+ players, his 93.7% accuracy means Portugal almost never lose the ball in the zone where the press should be most effective.
GS3 · 3 modes of scoring
Ronaldo covers all 3 within a single player — unique in the dossier
Ronaldo at 41 is the only player across all six teams who credibly covers all three scoring modes from a single position: 6-yd (42% — headed and redirected near-post), penalty-box (36% — instinctive half-volleys, penalty), and outside-box (22% — specifically free-kicks, where his dead-ball accuracy remains elite). This means a defence cannot position for "Ronaldo shots" — they have to account for all three zones simultaneously. Ramos reinforces the 6-yd mode. Leão provides the outside-to-inside third mode from a different vector. The shot discipline comparison: Ronaldo at 72% accuracy applies NBA-equivalent shot selection to a sport that doesn't require it. He almost never shoots from poor locations, which is the primary reason his xG overperformance has been consistent for 20 years.
GS4 · Comeback dynamics — lens
Portugal trailing: system transforms. Portugal leading: Deschamps-adjacent low block.
Football's lower contingency cost means Portugal trailing 1-0 in the 70th minute is a structurally different game than basketball trailing by 4 with 4 minutes left. Portugal trailing: Ronaldo's experience of exactly this scenario (multiple WC and UCL comeback situations) makes the team functionally calmer. Vitinha's pausa discretion accelerates — shorter hold times, more direct line-breaking passes. Leão's sprint budget management changes — he uses his saved sprint capacity for high-intensity bursts. Portugal leading: Martínez historically drops to a 4-4-2 mid-block, conserving Leão's sprints and using Neves + João Neves as defensive double pivot. This is the Deschamps-adjacent approach — accept low xG against, one counter-attack to seal it. The risk: against teams comfortable playing into a mid-block (Spain, France), Portugal leading can become Portugal under siege.

A1 · Path dependency count
Portugal's clean shot requires ~5/11 — reduced by Vitinha switch play
Standard state: 7 players must be correctly positioned for a Portugal clean shot. With Vitinha switch: ball movement reduces this to ~5 — the two wide defenders are repositioning during ball travel. With Ronaldo gravity active: reduces to ~4-5 — the CB shadowing Ronaldo cannot also track Ramos' late run. Minimum path dependency achieved: Leão 1v1 with fullback after diagonal switch — genuinely 2-player dependency (Leão beats man + GK save required). This is the lowest path-dependency Portugal can achieve and their peak zone is specifically designed to create it.
A2 · Touch economy
Average touches regain-to-shot: ~7 (Vitinha-triggered) vs ~3 (direct transition)
Portugal's structured build-up: approximately 7 touches from regain to shot attempt (Neves recycles → Vitinha carries → Fernandes receives → Leão receives → drives → shoots). Their direct transition: 3 touches (João Neves wins ball → Vitinha one-touch forward → Leão receives in stride → shoots). The direct transition generates higher xG per shot because Leão arrives at full sprint with fewer defenders in position. Martínez's tactical challenge: the 7-touch build-up is more controllable and possession-safe, the 3-touch transition is more dangerous but requires João Neves to be in the right press position first.
A3 · Sprint budget management
Ronaldo: walk-to-burst elite. Leão: high off-ball sprint volume, watch fatigue in mins 65-80.
Ronaldo's sprint budget management is the most sophisticated in any striker at this tournament — at 41 he has perfected the walk-to-burst cycle. His 30% fewer sprints/90 vs his prime is compensated by arriving at the burst moment at full neuromuscular capacity. First touch sharpness in the box does not degrade through 90 minutes as a result. Leão operates opposite: high total sprint volume (35km/h top speed, frequent on-ball bursts). His shot quality metrics in minutes 65-80 of matches show a measurable degradation — contact quality on the cut-inside shot drops when his sprint debt is high. Portugal managing Leão's minutes in the group stage is a tournament decision, not a rotation one.
A4 · Blocker type encountered
Leão faces: FB + last-line CB. Ronaldo faces: CB + GK (aerial). Vitinha-initiated: removes DM blocker.
For Portugal's primary sequences, the blocker types encountered at the shot moment differ systematically. Leão's left-channel shot: encounters fullback (already beat or bypassed) + last-line CB at angle (the awkward-angle blocker). Ronaldo's 6-yd delivery: encounters CB marking + goalkeeper (the final blocker). Ramos' near-post: encounters last-line CB + GK positioning. The Vitinha pausa specifically eliminates the DM blocker from Leão's path — by occupying the DM's attention with the hold, Leão's channel run is one blocker lighter. xG adjusted for blocker type: Leão's cut-inside shot from the entry angle faces 1 blocker (CB at angle) after Vitinha's pausa — this is a materially higher quality chance than the raw shot location suggests.

PITCH PLACEHOLDER · ENG GRAVITY MAP · THREE LAYERS
Kane's gravity works differently to Messi's (read-based) or Vinicius' (pace-based). Kane's gravity is hold-up based — his ability to receive with his back to goal, control, and lay off makes every defender within 5m of him engage, because releasing Kane in a half-turn inside the box is almost always fatal. This forces 2 defenders to commit to him in the box. Bellingham arriving from 25m cannot be tracked by those defenders. The three-layer gravity: Kane (box) → Bellingham (arriving) → Saka (wide) operates sequentially: one defender peels off Kane toward Bellingham, one toward Saka, and Kane is now in space for the return pass.
England's gravity is system-level rather than individual-genius-level — no single England player draws markers the way Messi does. But the Kane-Bellingham-Saka three-layer system creates sequential defensive decisions that compound. Each correct decision creates a new problem. The Miami Heat 2023 parallel holds: England are the team who won't be outscored in a shootout — their gravity system is pragmatic and high-efficiency, not beautiful.

GS1–GS4 · Game state summary
Kane's hold-up maximises spatial advantage for late runners. Rice's 180 recoveries are the discretion engine. Comeback capacity untested at this level.

The ball's next travel is 10-15m to Bellingham's late run — minimal travel time, maximum movement. This is the inverse of Portugal's long switch: England compress the ball-travel distance to maximise player-movement value during the hold phase.

GS2 Pausa discretion: Rice's 180 recoveries represent 180 discretion moments: each one is hold-and-recycle vs immediate forward. His 88.2% accuracy confirms he almost never makes the wrong choice. The discretion quality: he reads Bellingham's run timing from 30m away and times the forward pass to arrive as Bellingham enters the defensive gap — same as Mac Allister for Argentina but from a deeper starting position.

GS3 Three modes: Kane covers modes 1 and 2 at the highest accuracy rate in the dossier. Bellingham covers modes 2 and 3 from late-arriving positions. Saka provides mode 3 from the right. Mode 1 pure 6-yd conversion is Kane's strongest zone — no other team's primary player has 56.3% shot accuracy from all locations. England's dead-ball mode (set pieces) is Rice's delivery to Kane's aerial — an additional mode-1 route no other team deploys as consistently.

GS4 Comeback dynamics: England trailing is psychologically complicated by history (multiple near-miss tournament exits). Tuchel's 8-game perfect qualifying record (8W, 0 conceded) removes some of that anxiety. Tactically: England trailing means Rice must press higher (leaving Mainoo alone as anchor — risk), Bellingham must take more direct shots rather than arriving (reduces timing quality), and Saka must create from wider rather than his preferred cut-inside position. England's best comeback scenario is a Rice-triggered counter at pace — not a possession-based build-up dismantling a deep block.
